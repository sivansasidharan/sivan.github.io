Everyday, we create 2.5 quintillion bytes of data–so much that 90% of the data in the world today has been created in the last two years alone .This data comes from everywhere: sensors used to gather climate information, posts to social media sites, digital pictures and videos, purchase transaction records, and cell phone GPS signals to name a few. This data is big data. ( Courtesy - IBM )


Getting Ready ?
  • Big Data is the business challenge of the 21st century, and 2012 was the year that the Big Data revolution rocked the C-suite.
  • To stay ahead of the herd, business innovators have to conquer the sheer volume and velocity of business and customer insights available to them – and analyze and leverage this data to better serve their markets.
  • 90% of the data in the world today was created in the last 2 years alone (diverse sources as weather, social media posts, digital pictures and videos, purchase transaction records and cell phone GPS signals)
  • C-suite’s resolution for 2013 ? Use the Big Data opportunity to trap, tame and train their data monsters.
 Re-Imagination Of Data


What is being done?


Find Experts or Build Them
  1. Ability to share insights fast and easily by building a crack team who’s passionate about the possibilities.
  2. Since pace of execution is key – as is responding to customers in real time – build / outsource to people who live, breathe and dream data.
Take Advantage of the Cloud
  1. Outsourcing and the cloud save non-tech companies from having to chase and curb ever-increasing complexity.  
  2. All they look into is - “Can you take in all my data? Can you process and execute the analytical model we want?”
Execute, Test, Measure
  1. Feedback from customers confirm hunches immediately, so you can re-tool in hours, not weeks or months.
  2. Testing allows you to base business decisions on data - not guesses.
  3. Helps in making us “fully redundant and self-healing,”
